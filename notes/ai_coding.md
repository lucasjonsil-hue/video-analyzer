A developer showcases a clothing organization app called 'Hangr' that he built using Claude (Anthropic's AI). The app features a Chrome extension that captures clothing items from online stores and saves them to a virtual closet. He demonstrates how it helps solve the problem of forgetting clothes found while online shopping.

The creator opens by mentioning Claude 5 just released and this clothing organizer was the first thing he built with it. He explains his personal pain point: finding clothes online but then forgetting about them. He demonstrates the Hangr app at wearhangr.com, showing how a Chrome extension captures clothing items directly from retailer sites like Abercrombie & Fitch. Once captured, items appear in a closet view with details like brand, category, color, size, materials, and wear log. The feed shows items organized with statuses: wishlist, purchased, owned, or passed. He also highlights social features where friends can like items and you can browse their closets, useful for gift-giving. He mentions he has been using it for a few days and finds it genuinely useful.

- Built with Claude 5 (Anthropic AI) as a first project
- Chrome extension captures clothing items from any retail website
- Virtual closet at wearhangr.com organizes items with detailed metadata
- Supports outfit creation by mixing and matching saved pieces
- Social features let you follow friends and see what they've liked/saved
- Solves the real problem of forgetting clothes discovered during online browsing
