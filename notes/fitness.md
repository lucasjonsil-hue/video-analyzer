The coach explains that carbs influence T4-to-T3 thyroid hormone conversion, helping keep the metabolism elevated. One high-carb day per week is included to boost leptin and thyroid output, preventing fat loss plateaus. A detailed infographic in the final frame compares constant low-carb dieting (causing leptin and T3 to crash, slowing metabolism) versus carb cycling (maintaining leptin and T3 levels, sustaining fat loss and energy).

- A flat calorie deficit alone is not optimal — total weekly calories should be strategically distributed across training and rest days
- Carb cycling (high carb on training days, low carb on rest days) helps maintain leptin and T3 thyroid hormone levels
- Carbs should be front-loaded around pre- and post-workout windows to maximize insulin sensitivity and nutrient partitioning
- One high-carb day per week can boost leptin and thyroid output, breaking through fat loss plateaus
- Client John achieved visible body recomposition in just 5 weeks using this optimized protocol
- Constant low carbs cause metabolic slowdown, fatigue, and fat loss stalls
